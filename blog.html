What Is Sattu — and Why Has Everyone Forgotten It?

Sattu is roasted gram flour — chana dal dry-roasted and stone-ground into a fine powder. That single process unlocks something remarkable: a nutritional profile that rivals the most expensive supplements on the market, at a fraction of the cost and none of the chemicals.

For millennia, sattu was the daily drink of farmers, soldiers, and laborers across Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, and Jharkhand. Mixed with water, a pinch of salt, and a squeeze of lemon, it kept people going through brutal summer heat and back-breaking field work. It's cooling, filling, and deeply satisfying — the original Indian protein shake, before that was even a category.

Then packaged food arrived. Bright cans, slick branding, and aggressive marketing pushed sattu to the back of the pantry. A generation grew up thinking "healthy drinks" meant Bournvita and "refreshment" meant cola. We lost something ancient. RISE is here to take it back.
